## Overview

Throughout my studies and professional experiences, I have often encountered strong opinions about consulting. Social media discussions frequently portray consultants as disconnected from technical work, focused primarily on presentations, or detached from the realities of implementation.

To explore whether these perceptions actually reflect reality, I hosted a podcast episode titled **“Debunking the Myths of Cybersecurity Consulting.”**

The episode brought together cybersecurity professionals and incoming graduates from EY to discuss their experiences working in cybersecurity consulting and to provide a more nuanced perspective on the profession.

## Challenging Common Misconceptions

One of the main goals of the episode was to challenge some of the most common assumptions about consulting.

The discussion explored topics such as:

- Social media criticism of consultancy
- Expectations versus reality
- Career development opportunities
- Workplace culture
- Learning and mentorship
- The role of technical expertise within consulting

Many of these conversations highlighted that consulting is often much more diverse than the stereotypes suggest.

## The Technical Side of Cybersecurity Consulting

One aspect that stood out throughout the discussion was the amount of technical depth involved in cybersecurity consulting roles.

While consulting certainly involves communication, stakeholder management, and strategic thinking, many cybersecurity consultants also work closely with technical assessments, security frameworks, risk management activities, and emerging technologies.

The panel discussed how the reality of cybersecurity consulting is often far more hands-on than many students initially expect.

For me personally, this topic resonates strongly because it aligns with my own interest in bridging technical cybersecurity knowledge with governance, risk, and compliance perspectives.

## Key Takeaways

Some of the biggest lessons from the conversation included:

- Consulting is highly people-oriented but still requires strong technical foundations.
- Learning opportunities can be accelerated through exposure to different clients and challenges.
- Career growth is often driven by curiosity, initiative, and adaptability.
- Good consultants are not simply advisors; they need to understand the environments they are helping secure.
- Strong communication skills are just as important as technical expertise.

## Why I Created This Episode

As a student transitioning into the professional world, I wanted to create a resource that provides a realistic perspective on cybersecurity consulting for other students and young professionals.

There is a lot of information online, but much of it focuses on extremes. This episode aimed to provide an honest discussion about the opportunities, challenges, and realities of working in cybersecurity consulting.

Hosting this conversation was a rewarding experience and gave me the opportunity to learn from professionals with different experiences and viewpoints.

## Final Thoughts

Cybersecurity consulting continues to evolve alongside the threat landscape, emerging technologies, and changing business expectations.

Whether you are a student, recent graduate, or simply curious about consulting, I hope this episode helps provide a more balanced perspective on what the profession actually looks like beyond the stereotypes.

A big thank you to all the panel members for sharing their experiences and insights throughout the discussion.
