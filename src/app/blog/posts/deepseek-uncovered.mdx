## Overview

On December 9th, I attended a Tech & Meet session at HOWEST called **“DeepSeek Uncovered: The Open-Source AI Challenger from the East.”** The event focused on DeepSeek, an open-source AI model developed in China that is rapidly gaining attention as a potential alternative to models such as GPT-4 and Claude.

The session explored what makes DeepSeek different, how it compares to existing AI models, and included several live demonstrations showcasing its reasoning and code generation capabilities.

## What is DeepSeek?

![DeepSeek presentation](/images/blog/deepseek-uncovered/event-photo-01.jpeg)

DeepSeek is part of a growing movement toward open-source artificial intelligence. Unlike many closed commercial models, DeepSeek aims to make advanced AI systems more transparent and accessible to developers, researchers, and organizations.

The model focuses heavily on:

- Multimodal AI capabilities
- Advanced reasoning
- Code generation
- Open-source accessibility
- Community-driven innovation

What stood out to me was how quickly open-source AI is evolving into a serious competitor within the global AI landscape.

## Why This Matters

One of the most interesting takeaways from the session was that the AI race is no longer only about technical performance. It is increasingly becoming a discussion around transparency, accessibility, trust, and control.

Open-source AI introduces important questions:

- Who controls advanced AI systems?
- How transparent should AI models be?
- Can open-source models compete with large commercial players?
- What are the cybersecurity and governance implications?

As someone working in cybersecurity and GRC, I found this perspective especially valuable. The rise of open-source AI creates opportunities for innovation and accessibility, but it also raises important discussions around risk, governance, and responsible usage.

## Personal Reflection

What I appreciated most about the event was seeing how AI discussions are evolving beyond hype and performance benchmarks. Sessions like this help create a broader understanding of the ecosystem surrounding AI, including the strategic, ethical, and security-related considerations.

It was also interesting to see live demonstrations of DeepSeek’s reasoning and coding capabilities, especially considering how quickly the open-source AI community is progressing.

Events like these are a great reminder of how important it is to continuously stay informed in technology and cybersecurity, especially in areas that are evolving as rapidly as artificial intelligence.

## Final Thoughts

The DeepSeek session offered an insightful look into how open-source AI is positioning itself within the global AI race. Whether models like DeepSeek will eventually rival commercial leaders remains to be seen, but one thing is clear: open-source AI is becoming impossible to ignore.

A big thank you to HOWEST and Dimitri Casier for organizing the session and creating a space for these conversations.
