## Overview

Yesterday, I attended a very interesting **Tech&Meet** session organized by **HOWEST University of Applied Sciences** focusing on **Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)**.

The session was presented by **Niels Desloover** and **Sandro Manzo**, both members of the **CyTRIS department** within the **Centre for Cybersecurity Belgium (CCB)**. Their insights provided a valuable practical perspective on cyber threat intelligence and complemented the CTI course they are currently teaching us this semester.

## Cyber Threat Intelligence in Practice

One of the most interesting aspects of the session was hearing how threat intelligence is applied in real-world environments to help strengthen Belgium’s cybersecurity landscape.

Rather than discussing CTI purely from a theoretical perspective, the speakers shared practical examples of how intelligence-driven initiatives contribute to protecting organizations and citizens against evolving cyber threats.

This made the session especially valuable because it highlighted the operational side of cyber threat intelligence and its direct impact on national cyber resilience.

![Cyber Threat Intelligence session](/images/blog/threats-to-tactics/event-photo-01.jpeg)

## Spear Warning & BAPS

Two initiatives that particularly stood out during the session were:

- **Spear Warning**
- **Belgian Anti-Phishing Shield (BAPS)**

It was fascinating to learn how these initiatives help identify and mitigate malicious activity within Belgian cyberspace.

The discussion around phishing detection, threat monitoring, and proactive intelligence sharing demonstrated how important collaboration and rapid information exchange are in defending against modern cyber threats.

## Why This Matters

Cyber threat intelligence plays a critical role in helping organizations move from reactive security toward a more proactive defense approach.

Sessions like this reinforce how cybersecurity is not only about technical tooling, but also about:

- Information sharing
- Threat analysis
- Coordination
- Contextual understanding
- Rapid response capabilities

As someone interested in both technical cybersecurity and GRC, I found it especially interesting to see how intelligence operations support broader cybersecurity strategies and decision-making.

![Threat intelligence discussion](/images/blog/threats-to-tactics/event-photo-02.jpeg)

## Final Thoughts

This session was a great reminder of how dynamic and collaborative the cybersecurity field truly is.

Hearing directly from professionals working within the Centre for Cybersecurity Belgium made the topic much more tangible and showed how threat intelligence can have a real impact on protecting digital ecosystems at a national level.

A big thank you to HOWEST, Niels Desloover, and Sandro Manzo for sharing their expertise and experiences.
