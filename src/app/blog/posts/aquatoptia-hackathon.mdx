## Overview

Last week, I had the opportunity to participate in my very first hackathon: **Hack The Future 2025**.

Together with **Moaaz Eljamous**, I took part in the **“Leviathan’s Ledger”** challenge organized by OnCore. It turned out to be an incredibly engaging and technically rewarding experience that pushed us to think creatively, collaborate efficiently, and build under pressure.

The challenge focused on transforming historical 19th-century sailing ledgers into structured and interactive data visualizations.

## The Challenge

The provided material consisted of digitized PDF sailing ledgers containing completely unstructured information such as:

- Coordinates
- Dates
- Weather observations
- Handwritten notes
- Ship log remarks

The real challenge was turning this fragmented historical information into something structured, understandable, and visually interactive.

![Hackathon challenge setup](/images/blog/aquatopia-hack-the-future/project-photo-01.jpg)

## Processing Historical Data with AI

To process the sailing logs, we used an open API key together with **GPT-5 Mini** to help interpret, clean, and structure the extracted ledger entries.

Once the data became more organized, we converted everything into **GeoJSON**, making it easier to parse geographically and visualize dynamically.

This allowed us to transform historical ship routes into interactive location-based data that could be explored visually.

## Building the Dashboard

After structuring the data, we developed an interactive dashboard using:

- **Vue.js**
- **Mapbox**
- **GeoJSON**
- **Flask**

The platform displayed ship routes on a 3D globe together with all associated historical metadata from the original ledgers.

One of the most satisfying parts of the challenge was seeing centuries-old maritime journeys visualized interactively in a modern web application.

## Result & Reflection

I’m proud to share that we secured **4th place** in the challenge with a score of **77%** — a result we were genuinely happy with, especially considering it was our first hackathon experience.

Beyond the ranking itself, the event was an amazing opportunity to:

- Collaborate intensively as a team
- Work under time pressure
- Build an end-to-end solution within a limited timeframe
- Explore AI-assisted data processing
- Gain hands-on experience with geospatial visualization technologies

## Final Thoughts

Hack The Future showed me how valuable hackathons can be for learning, experimentation, and growth.

The combination of AI, historical data processing, geospatial visualization, and collaborative problem solving made this challenge both technically interesting and genuinely enjoyable to work on.

A huge thank you to the organizers, mentors, and everyone involved in creating such an inspiring environment. I’m already looking forward to participating in more hackathons and continuing to grow through these experiences.
